Business Analytics & Intelligence Information Systems and Technology, B.S.B.A.

Modified

Old Dominion University offers a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration in Information Systems and Technology with a major in Business Analytics & Intelligence. Students learn to develop decision models and use computers to manipulate and analyze data — enhancing decision-making in a business environment. Old Dominion University offers a Bachelor of Science in Business Administration degree in information systems and technology with a major in business analytics and intelligence. The major in Business Analytics and Intelligence enables students to properly develop decision models and use computers to manipulate and analyze data in order to enhance decision making in a business environment.

Graduates work at the intersection of business analytics — where organizations turn data into actionable insight — and information systems — where the platforms that collect and analyze that data are designed and built. They act at the intersection of business analytics, where organizations turn data into valuable and actionable insights, and information systems, designing and implementing the IT systems necessary to collect and analyze data.

Featured Courses

Three courses that anchor the major.

Every course is taught in the ODU business school and mapped to a specific analytics skill. Full sequences and prerequisites are in the university catalog.

Modified
BNAL 403

Data Visualization and Exploration

An applied treatment of how to translate raw datasets into visual explanations that support business decisions.

This course is part of the Day One Access program; you should not purchase any course materials denoted by the Day One Access logo as they are part of your course registration.

IT 363

Systems Analysis and Design

An introduction to the analysis and design of computer-based information systems. Emphasis is placed upon developing requirements that serve the business needs of the organization, and on the logical and physical design of business information systems.

Covers structured and object-oriented approaches to system analysis and design, including software development methodologies, requirement gathering, modeling, and design techniques. Prerequisites: IT 201, IT 205, and declared major in Strome College of Business.

IT 410

Business Intelligence

Business intelligence, data warehouse, data mining, and OLAP. Uses state-of-the-art business intelligence software tools, including SAS products, to provide hands-on experience designing and using data warehouses.

Prerequisite: BNAL 206.
